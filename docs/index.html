Introduction

Camera Obscura, also known as a pinhole image, is is the natural optical phenomenon that occurs when an image of a scene at the other side of a screen (or for instance a wall) is projected through a small hole in that screen as a reversed and inverted image (left to right and upside down) on a surface opposite to the opening.

Step 1 : The box

We will cover the five faces of the inside of the cardboard with black paper and the remaining one side with white paper. The side with white paper will be our screen. Then we will seal the cardboard with black duck tape. Make sure that all the corners and margins are properly sealed to prevent all lighting from going into the cardboard.

Step 2 : Pinhole

We will cut our a large hole in our cardboard and cover this hole with card paper. We've designed a slider slot so that we can insert card paper with pinholes with different diameters. We made pinhole sliders with diameters 0.8mm, 3mm, and 5mm. We've calculated and decided on the diameter of 0.8mm by calculating using $$d = 1.9\cdot\sqrt{f\cdot\lambda} = 1.9\cdot\sqrt{(360mm)\cdot(550\cdot10^{-6}mm)} = 0.845mm$$
